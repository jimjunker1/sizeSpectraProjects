Results

Total community abundances ranged ~3 order of magnitude from 1.1e+04 to 6.4e+06 with average community abundance of 7.8e+05 \(\pm\) 9.9e+05 individuals m-2. Community mean body sizes exhibited a similar range from 1.1e-01 to 1.4e+02 with an average community mean body size of 3.34 \(\pm\) 12.71 mg dry mass individual-1.

The cross community relationship between community mean body size and total community abundance exhibited no evidence of clear relationship between these variables (Figure 1). The median slope estimate between \(log_{10} \overline{M^{0.75}}\) and \(log_{10} N_{total}\) was 0.02 (-0.12–0.16; 95% credible interval).

Appendix

Body size treatment

While the calculation of total abundance is straightforward, estimating mean body size of the community can be done in multiple ways: 1) by taking the mean of all individuals (i.e., abundance-weighted mean), by calculating the relative biomass of each body size and using this to weight body size by relative biomass (i.e., biomass-weighted), and lastly to correct each mass to metabolic mass (M0.75) and take the abundance- or biomass-weighted mean of these corrected body sizes. The theorized relationship between abundance and body size in \(N \propto M\) is based on energy use, i.e. metabolism, and metabolism scales non-linearly with body size, therefore, the metabolic correction is most appropriate [@white2007]. These differences can have significant influence on the community biomass estimates (Figure S1).
