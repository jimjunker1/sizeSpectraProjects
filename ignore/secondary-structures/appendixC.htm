Appendix C. Statistical arguments for the two-step fitting procedure to extract secondary structure of ISD.

Here, we show why sequential two-step fitting for log ISD (fitting power-law first and then the secondary structure) is a better strategy than one-step fitting (simultaneously fitting both), if the goal is to extract the secondary structure of ISD without sacrificing the ecological implication of the power-law component. For a statistical reason, the estimation of the power-law exponent is influenced by the estimation of secondary structure. To illustrate this, we compare the results of one-step simultaneous fitting vs. our two-step procedure (Table C1). On one hand, the estimate of the power-law exponent from the one-step fitting is very different from that of the two-step procedure (Table C1); moreover, the estimated exponent from the one-step simultaneous fitting deviates substantially from either the theoretical inferences (Kerr and Dickie 2001) or common empirical measurements, which is around -1. Some of these estimated exponents are even positive, which is very unlikely in biological sense. On the other hand, the estimated 2nd and 3rd order coefficients also showed subtle difference between the two types of fittings. However, the estimations obtained from the two-step fitting are neither systematically larger nor smaller than the one-step fitting. Thus, the two-step fitting does not magnify the magnitude of the secondary structure comparing with the one-step fitting.

The inter-dependence among the estimations of coefficients can be explained by the theory of statistical linear model (Sen and Srivastava 1990). For example, in a model that y is simultaneously fitted by the partial regression coefficients of xi, βi, is not equal to the coefficients estimated from the total regression model y ~ xi. Instead, the partial regression coefficient βi equals to the coefficient estimated from the following model: y ~ ei, where ei is the residuals of the fitting, . According to this argument, the estimation of power-law exponent can be influenced by the secondary structure in one-step fitting, which makes the ecological implication of the power-law component much different from what have been proposed in the previous studies. Therefore, to keep the biological sense of the power-law component, it is better to separate the power-law from log ISD first. While, the left polynomial secondary structure can still be thought as a unity to infer the complexity of food web, unless the biological meaning of the 2nd order coefficient can be clearly recognized and distinguished from the 3rd or higher order coefficient in future studies.
