Appendix K. The prediction of the generalized MTE model under nonequilibrium state caused by disturbances.

The disturbances, including typhoons and overturns (Fig. E1), may potentially cause the plankton community to deviate from steady state. Here, we quantified the degree of deviation from steady state (the degree of nonequilibrium) as the proportion of data points (number of samplings) in a given K-means group that was within five weeks (Fig. K1A), six weeks (Fig. K1B), or seven weeks (Fig. K1C) after any typhoon or overturn event. Here, we tried various return times: 5, 6, or 7 weeks. We do not know the return time of the whole plankton community in response to disturbance in Feitsui Reservoir. Nevertheless, the return times are chosen according to the previous mesocosm study (Cottingham et al. 2004) suggesting that the return time of plankton community responding to nutrient pulses and biomass change is up to 40 days since disturbances had happened. As can be seen in Fig. K1, a positive relationship exists between the degree of non-steady state and the standardized deviations from the prediction of general MTE model.
