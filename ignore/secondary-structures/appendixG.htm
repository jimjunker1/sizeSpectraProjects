Appendix G. Time-integrated secondary structure of ISD over season.

The secondary structure is a stable feature of ISD in a longer (seasonal) scale (Table G1). The stability of secondary structure in time suggests that a majority of the secondary structure is raised due to the deterministic trophic structure rather than just transient dynamics of ISD. The magnitude of time-integrated secondary structure was not smaller than that calculated from individual samples (comparing Table G1 with Fig. 2). However, we still cannot rule out the possibility that some parts of the secondary structure were resulted from transient dynamics, especially when the disturbance were strong so that trophic structure cannot recover to an equilibrium state in our sampling time scale around two weeks. See our analysis on this issue in Appendix K.
