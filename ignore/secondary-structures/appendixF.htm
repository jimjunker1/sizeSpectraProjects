Appendix F. Criteria to select the cubic polynomial function.

We explain why the cubic is the most suitable order for the polynomial fitting in this study from statistical and ecological perspectives. From a statistical perspective, in most of cases, cubic polynomial fits data better than the quadratic model (Table F1). All the polynomial secondary structure of ISD has the lowest AIC when fitted with cubic polynomial. In addition, half of size-TL relationships also demonstrate the lowest AIC when fitted with cubic polynomial. None of size-TL relationship can be fitted better by a forth order model because of limited number of sampled size fractions. Moreover, specifying the consistent cubic model is necessary for testing our theoretical predictions because we try to compare the same order size coefficients between size-TL relationship and the secondary structure of ISD. Here, we choose the cubic model because it can cover the estimation of lower order polynomial (e.g., quadratic).

From the ecological perspectives, we can see that the cubic model is reasonable because there are two potential trophic interactions in our lake system: omnivory and microbial interactions. These trophic interactions may increase the nonlinearity of size-TL relationship in different size ranges, and then create at least two “curves” in the size-TL relationship. Note that, we suggest that the order of the polynomial secondary structure may not be fixed among different systems because different food webs may have different ways of trophic interactions that cause nonlinearity.
